About

The SMARTSchemist server shall enable anyone to competently and adequatly discuss the chemistry of molecules. This is done by matching common substructures and highlighting them including their (trivial) names. The substructures highlighted are common functional groups, small (hetero)cycles and some biologically relevant molecules.
As always, there are multiple different resources we used to build the SMARTSchemist, namely rdkit for the cheminformatics platform to enable doing this completely open source, a publication of Ihlenfeldt et. al. as a starting point for common functional groups (heavily altered) and PubchemPy which was used to automatically name generated heterocycles.
The SMARTSchemist is the brain child of Torben Gutermuth (main Developer, Pattern Generation), Jochen Sieg (Developer) and Patrick Penner (Early Planning) and was developed during their respective times as PHD student in the group of Matthias Rarey in Hamburg.
